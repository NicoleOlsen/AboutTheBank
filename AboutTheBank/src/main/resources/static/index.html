Financial products

Securities

Direct financing instruments of companies, banks, states or public entities. Can be traded over the counter or through organised markets. Are negotiable instruments (secondary market).

Medium term note/Commercial paper (share of a medium or short-term claim) Bond (share of a long term claim) Share/Equities (share in the capital of a company)

Balance sheet transactions

Transactions involving an immediate or deferred recognition in the balance sheet. These products are traded only over the counter.

Cash loans/borrowings Currency transactions (Spot, Future)

Derivatives and options

Security with a value that is derived from, an underlying asset (or group of assets), with other words, price is determined by fluctuations in the underlying asset. It is not recorded in the balance sheet of the financial institution. It is traded on organized markets or over the counter

OTC (non-standardized contract) Contract to buy or to sell an asset at in advance agreed date and price FX (foreign exchange or currency derivative)

Structured Products

- combination of two or more financial instruments
1. Structured capital-guaranteed products
2. Structured capital-at-risk products
savings or investment products where the return is linked to an underlying asset with pre-defined features structured products represent an original and effective alternative to the usual financial investments. They offer solutions that can be adapted to the needs of each investor, for example in terms of strategy, risk/return profile, maturity or the amount to be invested. They enable investments in a wide range of underlying assets (equities, interest rates, foreign exchange, indices, commodities ...) and offer various redemption possibilities. tailored to meet your needs. No other product category offers you such a wide variety of investment solutions for every imaginable market scenario
