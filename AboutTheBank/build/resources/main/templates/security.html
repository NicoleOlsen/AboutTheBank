Bond

  • Form of borrowing, where lender receives periodic interests and the amount borrowed.
  • CHARACTERISTICS
    fixed-income securities - lender can anticipate the exact amount of cash received
    low risk - in case of bankruptcy, bondholders must be repaid from a liquidation in full before any shareholders are paid anything

    EXAMPLE
    goverment needs money for roads and the amount needed is higher than a bank can provide

    ATTRIBUTES
    buyer = lender
    face value = amout borrowed
    maturity date = date on which the issuer must repay the amount borrowed
    interest - with rate and schedule (interest if often referred to as bondâ€™s yield or coupon)
    - fixed interest
    - variable rate (calculated on predetermined spread above some benchmark)

    When the bond matures in a decade, you'd then get your $1,000 back.

Share

  • Units of ownership interest in a corporation or financial asset that provide for an equal distribution in any profits, if any are declared, in the form of dividends.
  • TYPES
    1. common shares: dividends not guaranteed (more risky), shareholders have voting rights
    2. preferred shares: dividend is paid out regularly (less risky) Preferred stock takes priority over common stock, if the business files for bankruptcy.
    Earnings per share (EPS) is the portion of a company's net income that would be earned per share if all the profits were paid out to its shareholders.
